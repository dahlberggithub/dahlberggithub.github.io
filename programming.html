Graph Theory Conjecture Resolution (Python)

Together with my two coauthors, Stephanie van Willigenburg and Angele Foley, we resolved a 20-year-old conjecture by Richard Stanley, a leader of combinatorics.

"We donâ€™t know of a graph which is not contractible to K13 (even regarding multiple edges of a contraction as a single edge) which is not e-positive."
-- Richard Stanley

By developeing Python code (specifically in SageMath) to we analyzed graphs with specific properties, those graphs that were claw-free, claw-contractible-free and also not e-positive. A full search was impossible due to time complexity. However, we optimized by targeting likely graphs and ultimately produced a counterexample! We published the results in a top academic journal that received over 35 citations, and earned the Mathematical Association of America (MAA) David P. Robbins Prize.

Publication: Resolving Stanley's e-positivity of claw-contractible-free graphs (with A. Foley and S. van Willigenburg). J. Eur. Math. Soc. (JEMS), 22:2673--2696, 2020.

Mystery Mosaics (JavaScript)

In this project you can upload an image and turn it into a mystery mosaic. This is a grid of boxes with numbers and a key to decode those numbers into colors. Color the boxes as indicated and discover the picture.
